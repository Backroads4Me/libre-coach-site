## Hardware Assembly & Wiring

These photos show the detailed process of assembling the RV Link hardware.

<CardGrid>
    <Card title="Start: Board Prep" icon="setting">

        Check your jumpers: 120 ohm resistor should be OFF for most integrations (Right jumper in correct position).
    </Card>
    <Card title="Thermal Management">
        <Image src={assembly8} alt="raspberry pi with the heat transfer pads before being installed on the pi" />
        Apply heat transfer pads to the Raspberry Pi.
    </Card>
    <Card title="NVMe Adapter">
        <Image src={assembly20} alt="top down view of the pi with the Waveshare PCIe To M.2 Adapter Board (E) installed and the standoffs installed in all 4 corners, no nvme installed" />
        Install the Waveshare PCIe To M.2 Adapter Board with standoffs.
    </Card>
    <Card title="NVMe Installation">
        <Image src={assembly21} alt="top down view of the pi with the Waveshare PCIe To M.2 Adapter Board (E) installed and the standoffs installed in all 4 corners with nvme installed" />
        Secure the NVMe drive to the adapter board.
    </Card>
    <Card title="CAN HAT Installation">
        <Image src={assembly22} alt="top down view of the Waveshare CAN HAT + installed on the top of the pi using the 4 standoffs" />
        Mount the Waveshare CAN HAT + on top of the stack.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Side View">
        <Image src={assembly23} alt="side view Waveshare CAN HAT + installed on the pi showing how the HAT header slides over the pi pins" />
        Ensure the GPIO header is fully seated.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Power Connection">
        <Image src={assembly24} alt="view of the PCIe To M.2 Adapter Board (E) power supply cable connected to the HAT" />
